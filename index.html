Non-Tech Track
Step 4 · AI Readiness

For non-tech learners, AI questions should focus on practice, not jargon.

We care about whether the learner has real hands-on usage, whether they have tried building anything, and what stops them from going deeper. We do not need them to speak like engineers to get a useful signal.

Prompting, workflow thinking, and simple vibe-coded experiments are meaningful signals here and should be treated as such.

A letter for your journey ahead

The system speaks differently here because the learner is earlier in the journey and needs confidence plus practical direction, not engineering-centric jargon.

Hi Ish,

You are entering this program at a moment where AI is opening doors for people who know how to think clearly, learn quickly, and turn tools into useful workflows.

The next few months will be about moving from curiosity to capability. That means building enough technical comfort to stay grounded, while also learning how to use AI in a way that creates real outcomes instead of only one-off experiments.

What we will expect from you is steady practice, visible output, and the willingness to stay with the process even before confidence catches up.
